How did SOPAJS know which state you were in? Are you spying on me?

SOPAJS isn't spying on you. The simple answer is that every time you connect to a website, you send along an "IP address" which tells the receiving server how to send information back to you. Since IP addresses are a limited resource and are distributed by a central authority, resources exist which allow us to figure out roughly where your IP address is located.

Note: the location is not very accurate. According to my IP address, SOPAJS thinks my home's IP address is from about 30 miles north. SOPAJS just uses the location to try to get you relevant content (e.g. your senators).

Why build this? Are you opposed to SOPA?

To be honest, I'm not hugely informed about SOPA, so can't oppose it strongly since that would be dishonest. That said, SOPA has caused quite a stir and I'm very much in favor of SOPA being debated.

SOPAJS is intended to encourage discussion by making people aware of SOPA and of those who are for and against SOPA. Hopefully, making more people aware of SOPA will assure that SOPA is a bill that is healthy for the online community.
